Sales Performance Dashboard

Problem Statement

The company struggled with:

  • Lack of Real-Time Visibility: Data silos caused reporting delays and inefficient trend tracking.
  • Inconsistent Growth Analysis: Year-over-year comparisons and margin trends were unclear.
  • Weak Product Insights: Revenue drivers and underperformers were hard to identify.
  • Market Blind Spots: No clear view of performance across regions.
  • Limited Customer Analysis: Retention and high-value customer behaviors were not tracked.

Need for Dashboards

To solve these issues, I built interactive Power BI dashboards that:

  • ‚úî Deliver real-time metrics on sales and margins.
  • ‚úî Enable year-over-year and YTD growth analysis.
  • ‚úî Drive decisions with product and region-level insights.
  • ‚úî Uncover customer behavior to boost retention.

Implementation Steps

  1. Data Loading: Connected CSV files in Power BI Desktop.
  2. Data Prep: Created Sales and Return tables; analyzed columns in Power Query.
  3. Modeling: Built a snowflake schema with 2 fact tables and 4 dimension tables.
  4. Visualization: Used KPI cards, charts, maps, tables, and slicers to reveal insights.
  5. UX Enhancements: Applied custom themes, buttons, bookmarks, tooltips, and dynamic titles.
  6. Drill-Throughs: Enabled deep-dives into order-level data.
  7. Filters: Added dynamic filtering using date tables and custom slicer panels.

Key Insights

1. Gross Margin YoY

  • 73.2% growth in gross margin ($48M vs. $28M).
  • Highest growth: Glutamine (+757.8%), Casein Protein (+3,306.6%).
  • Underperformers: Jackets (0% growth), Vests (+6.5%).

2. Sales Performance

  • Net Sales: $72M | Orders: 3,796 | Customers: 635.
  • Protein dominates ($64M), while Vitamins underperform.
  • Most sales from North America; growth potential in South America, Asia, Africa.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Double Down on Winners: Expand Protein & Carbs lines: Increase marketing efforts for Protein and Carbs, as they are driving the highest revenue and growth. Expand product lines within these categories to capture more market share
  • Fix the Gaps: Rethink strategy for Vitamins & Clothing : Improve the visibility and pricing strategy for Vitamins and Clothing, particularly Jackets and Vests. Consider bundling promotions to boost sales in slow-moving categories.
  • Capitalize on Peaks: Boost inventory & marketing in May, July, October: Plan inventory, promotions, and marketing campaigns around these periods to maximize revenue.
  • Go Global: Target growth markets with tailored campaigns: Sales are primarily concentrated in North America and Europe, while South America has minimal sales. Explore growth opportunities in emerging markets like Asia and Africa through strategic partnerships.
  • Build Loyalty: Leverage strong customer retention with loyalty programs: With 3,796 orders and 635 customers, the company has a good repeat purchase rate. Implement a loyalty program or subscription model to enhance long-term customer value.

Conclusion

Overall, the dashboards indicate strong business performance, significant YoY growth, and promising expansion opportunities. However, category-level optimization and market expansion are crucial to sustaining this momentum..

Sales Diagnostic Analysis

Product Performance Diagnostic Analysis

üìå Business Problem

The gross margin dropped below 30% in the Southeast region. The business wants to identify the product category and subcategory responsible and understand why it happened.

üîç Key Findings

  • Category: Protein
  • Subcategory: Whey Protein
  • Region: Southeast
  • Period: May & June
  • Gross Margin: -127.79%

üßÆ Cost vs. Price Analysis

Month Avg Unit Cost Avg Unit Price Loss per Unit
May $305.80 $158.61 $147.19
June $305.19 $145.55 $159.64

üìâ Root Cause

Unit cost was significantly higher than the selling price in both months, which is abnormal and caused heavy losses.

üìà Recommendations

  • Temporarily pause Whey Protein sales in Southeast.
  • Audit cost inputs and review supplier or logistics pricing.
  • Update product pricing to prevent cost > price situations.
  • Implement margin alerts in Power BI (e.g., margin < 30%).
  • Explore bundling or repositioning strategies to improve margins.

‚úÖ Conclusion

The margin drop is caused by misaligned pricing for Whey Protein in the Southeast. With cost control, pricing corrections, and better analytics, profitability can be restored.
